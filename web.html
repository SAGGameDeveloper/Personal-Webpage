ABOUT

Hello! My name is Sergio, I'm a freelancer developer and translator. I started programming at the age of 11, driven just by curiosity. I couldn't but love it, from that moment on I have been programming non-stop. For some years, I dedicated myself to videogames development, experimenting with different environments and languages.

Some years ago, I expanded my horizons and became interested in Android development and web development. That allowed me to adquire a much deeper knowledge of the technologies used nowadays. This is why I can move both on front-end and back-end fields, allowing me to become a Full Stack developer.

While learning to program, I began to build a strong relationship with the english language. I started to use it for everything, until I came to the point where I had the ability to translate everything I wanted with no effort, so I started working as a translator.

WORK



Personal Webpage

This own site is, of course, one of my works as a programmer and web-developer. I find having a personal website is one of the best things you can do as a freelancer. It's the entry point from which people can know who you are, what you do and how to get in touch.

Hence, the moment I went freelancer, the first obvious thing to do was this webpage. I approached it from a modern perspective; being elegance, minimalism and responsiveness the main pillars of the project.



The Tryangle

This was the first project I ended as a programmer. I started it when I was 14 years old and finished half a year later, working only in my spare time. Initially, it was done using GameMaker, but in the middle of the development, I decided to move the whole game to Unity. Therefore, I learned C# and remaked the game from scratch using the [new] 2D features of Unity. I finally decide to publish it at the age of 17.

Given the age I was and the experience I had when I did it; nor the design, nor the graphic design, nor the programming is an actual representation of my current abilities. Still, for the knowledge I had at the time, I find it to be an excelent proof of my vocation and dedication.



Kid Icarus with Javascript

While on college, a friend of mine and me decided to do a Kid Icarus reduced version. To do so, we used HTML, Javascript, Phaser and NodeJS. The whole project is available and open on Github, where you can also play it directly through Gihub Pages.

The main focus of the development is on the programming part, as we were not game designers and that was not the point of the project. Being this so, our first priority was to make a solid and scalable software arquitecture while learning more about the technologies we were using.



Videogames journalism

I have always been a huge lover of videogames and literature. Mixing those two at some point seemed to be a logical step on my path. Some random day, I found the opportunity to make a trial for a videogames digital magazine, no doubt I had to try. After passing, I started working as a videogames journalist.

After 3 months, I found that was not what I have expected, so I quited. Even so, while working there I learned quite a lot about writing and delivering good quality content. Moreover, I had to translate quite a lot from english [news, declarations, interviews] to spanish, adquiring then solid translation skills.

Here you have one of my finest works.
